## Positioning (read before writing anything)

**What HW Media is:** a London creative agency and production company that makes high-end films for premium brands. Director-led, small by design, working worldwide.

**What it is not:** "a guy with a camera." Never frame it as one person. Harry leads it, but the language is **we / the agency / the crew**. The team and the collective do the work.

**The feel:** expensive without saying expensive. Confident, quiet, specific. The kind of company McLaren and Aston Martin already trust — because they do. Proof over adjectives: real clients, real numbers, real production detail.

**Voice rules:**
- Short sentences. Plain words. No agency jargon ("storytelling solutions", "content ecosystems" — banned).
- Claims must be concrete: shoot days, crew sizes, turnarounds, client names.
- First person plural. "We find the story." Never "I".
- One idea per sentence. If a line needs reading twice, rewrite it.
- No em dashes in prose.

**Brand lines (verbatim, from Harry's own materials):**
- Motto: **"Break the ordinary."** (Instagram bio, all his materials)

### SC.03 — The method (edit timeline)

- **H2:** How a film gets made.
- **Lead:** Five stages, one crew, no handovers. Drag the playhead with your scroll and watch the job move through the timeline.
- **Red script:** scroll = scrub the edit ⟶ · the fun bit (on clip 03)
- **Steps:**
  1. **Development** — We find the story before anything is booked. What's true about your brand, and why would anyone care? That answer becomes the treatment.
  2. **Pre-production** — Boards, locations, crew, weather windows. The boring week that makes the shoot calm. Most problems get solved here, before they exist.
  3. **Production** — The shoot. One crew, every department, a plan for the day and room to chase the better shot when it shows up.
  4. **Post** — The edit, the grade, the sound. This is where footage becomes a film, and it gets as much care as the shoot did.
  5. **Delivery** — Masters, plus every cutdown and format your channels need. Nothing cropped as an afterthought. You get the lot.
